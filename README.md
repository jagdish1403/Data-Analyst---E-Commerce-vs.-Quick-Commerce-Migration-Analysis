# E-Commerce vs. Quick Commerce: Customer Migration Analysis 🛒⚡

## 📌 Project Overview
The retail landscape is rapidly shifting with the rise of Quick Commerce (Q-Comm) platforms like Zepto and Swiggy Instamart. This project analyzes customer purchasing behavior to understand why users are migrating away from traditional E-Commerce giants (like Amazon) toward Q-Comm alternatives. 

By analyzing transactional data, delivery times, and customer demographics, this project quantifies the business impact of this shift—specifically focusing on order drop-offs and estimated revenue loss—and provides data-driven recommendations for customer retention.

## 🎯 Objectives
* Identify the primary reasons customers switch to Quick Commerce.
* Analyze the correlation between delivery time differences and customer churn.
* Quantify the estimated revenue lost by traditional e-commerce platforms.
* Evaluate how discounts, geographic locations (City Tiers), and order time slots influence platform preference.

## 📊 Dataset Description
The analysis is based on a comprehensive dataset containing customer transaction records, including:
* **Demographics:** City Tier, Region, Age Group, Income Bracket.
* **Order Metrics:** Amazon vs. Q-Comm Order Values, Delivery Times, Discount Percentages.
* **Switching Behavior:** Reasons for switching, Q-Comm Platform Chosen, Monthly Order Drop.
* **Business Impact:** Amazon Estimated Revenue Lost, Cart Abandonment Rates, Net Promoter Scores (NPS).

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies Used
* **Data Cleaning & Transformation:** SQL / Pandas (Python)
* **Data Visualization & Dashboards:** Power BI / Excel
* **Spreadsheet Analysis:** Microsoft Excel

## 💡 Key Insights (Example)
* **Speed is King:** The largest driver for switching is "Speed/Delivery Time," with customers opting for Q-Comm when the delivery difference exceeds [X] minutes.
* **Revenue Impact:** Customers who installed a Q-Comm app showed an average decline of [X]% in their monthly traditional e-commerce orders, leading to significant cart abandonment.
* **Segment Vulnerability:** Young Professionals in Tier-1 cities ordering in the "Morning (6-10AM)" slot are the most likely demographic to switch.
